Distributed Intelligence Initiative

A reliable floor of AI that can’t be switched off

Capable open-weight AI running on hardware people own — local-first, pooled into trusted pods, federated with no central hub.

Local-first · works offline No single off-switch AI for All, in the spirit of Tor Conceptual overview · early-stage, subject to change
1 Your machine serves it → 2 else a trusted pod peer → 3 else a federated pod → ✦ degrade gracefully — never to zero

The Node

One person’s machine running a capable open model (14–30B). Fully useful offline — the smallest thing that delivers the whole promise to a single user.

The Pod

A few nodes that already trust each other — your machines, a lab, a library, a town. A pod may open its door to serve consumers who belong to no pod.

The Consumer

A phone or browser with no model — and no pod. It reaches whichever public-serving pod can take it. The many are served by the few.

The Federation

Pods peer with other pods through signed, gossiped discovery. No central hub, no registry, nothing a single directive can switch off.

The Overflow Path

Work stays local, and only spills outward when needed: local, then pod, then federation — each hop within policy you control.

The idea, in one line: where Tor is privacy for all, DII is intelligence for all — volunteer-run, resilient by design, free to use whether or not you contribute.
