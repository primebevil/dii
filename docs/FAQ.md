# FAQ

**Is this decentralized AI?**
It is an open-source research initiative into keeping capable, open-weight AI available to everyone on hardware they own, resilient by design and beyond the reach of any single off-switch.

**Are you competing with the frontier labs?**
No. The goal is a reliable floor of useful intelligence, meaning advanced-enough models that are always available. Frontier parity is a separate problem.

**Is this a business? Is there a token?**
No and no. It is free and non-commercial by commitment, with no business model, token, credit-currency, or paid marketplace. The model is Tor: a mission-driven volunteer effort, not a company (ADR-0005).

**Why would I run a node, then?**
For the same reason people run a Tor relay: because you want the thing to exist. You run a node so your community, the people around you, and those who cannot self-host keep access to a reliable floor of intelligence that no one can switch off. It is mission and community, not payment. Access is never gated on contribution, so most people who benefit will never run a node, and that is by design. A light, non-transferable priority signal for contributors under congestion may exist later, but it is a minor fairness detail, not the reason to take part (ADR-0002, ADR-0005).

**Is it like Tor?**
In spirit, yes. Where Tor is privacy for all, DII is AI for All: distributed, volunteer-run, resilient against a single off-switch, and free to use whether or not you contribute. The analogy is about motivation and resilience, not the technology; DII does not use onion routing.

**Does it work offline?**
Yes. Local-first is the whole point. A single node with no network still delivers capable AI, and the network adds overflow capacity when it is available.

**Why does this matter now?**
See [Why DII Exists](./Why_DII_Exists.md). In June 2026 a frontier model was switched off worldwide by directive, then switched back on two weeks later, which proved that access to centralized models can be revoked at will.
