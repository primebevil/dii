# Contributing

This is a free, open, RFC-style research project. Contributions of research,
architecture, and prototypes are welcome.

- Cite sources.
- Label hypotheses as hypotheses.
- Record experiments and decisions (ADRs), and explain *why*.
- Prefer evidence over assumptions; let evidence change conclusions.
- Keep the reliable-floor mission in view: local-first, open weights, no single
  point of control.

## How this project is authored

This project is AI-drafted and human-directed, and I want that stated plainly. I
set the direction, make the decisions, and review and approve everything that
lands here; an AI assistant does most of the research, drafting, and building
under that direction, and I stay in the loop as the reviewer and decision-maker.
This is a deliberate way of working, not a shortcut. The scarce resource is my
judgment and review, so the documents are written to be read and checked, and
load-bearing claims are meant to trace back to a source.

That is also why the records read the way they do. Journals are my reflective
log of the work. ADRs state decisions and the reasoning behind them. Research
write-ups are checked against their sources, with an independent verification
pass and a validation checklist, precisely because the drafting is assisted and
the claims have to stand on their own.
